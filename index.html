Your Healing Goals

This guide is designed to help you clarify what you'd like to change and begin to identify old beliefs that may be holding you back. Think of this as a way to get curious about your inner world and create a roadmap for our work together. There are no right or wrong answers, and we can always explore this in our sessions if it feels overwhelming.

What I'd Like to Change

Behavior

What are you doing that you don't want to do?

What are you not doing (or not doing enough) that you do want to do?

Emotions

What are you feeling that you don't want to feel?

What are you not feeling (or not feeling as much as you'd like) that you do want to feel?

Sensations

What are you feeling (physically) that you don't want to feel?

What are you not feeling (physically) that you do want to feel?

Thoughts

What are you thinking that you don't want to think (believe)?

What are you not thinking that you do want to think (believe)?

Examples of Negative Beliefs

Sometimes it can be difficult to name the beliefs that hold us back. This list can help you get a sense of what might be coming up for you. We can use these as a starting point to identify the core beliefs we'll work on together.

Responsibility (I am defective)

  • I don't deserve love
  • I am a bad person
  • I am ugly (my body is hateful)
  • I do not deserve...
  • I am terrible
  • I am stupid (not smart enough)
  • I am worthless (inadequate)
  • I am insignificant (unimportant)
  • I am shameful
  • I am a disappointment
  • I am not lovable
  • I am not good enough
  • I deserve to die
  • I deserve to be miserable
  • I deserve only bad things
  • I am different (don't belong)
  • I am permanently damaged

Responsibility (I did something wrong)

  • I should have done something
  • I should have known better
  • I did something wrong

Safety/Vulnerability

  • I cannot be trusted
  • I cannot trust myself
  • I cannot trust my judgment
  • I am in danger
  • It's not OK to feel (show) my emotions
  • I cannot trust anyone
  • I cannot protect myself
  • I cannot stand up for myself
  • I cannot let it out

Control/Choices

  • I am not in control
  • I cannot do...
  • I am powerless (helpless)
  • I am weak
  • I cannot get what I want
  • I cannot succeed
  • I have to be perfect (please everyone)
  • I cannot stand it
  • I am a failure (will fail)

Remember that this is a guide, not a test. We will use this information to begin the process of understanding and transforming these old beliefs. The bravest step is simply being willing to look at them.
